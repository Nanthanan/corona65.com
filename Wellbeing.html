The Environmental determinants of Diabetes in the Young (TEDDY) Study is an international study that has produced data used by scientists to create a test that combines several different factors that indicate whether a child is likely to develop type 1 diabetes. Are You Immune System Aware?

by Colin Leadbetter

Immunity:
it seems that now, more than ever, we are lookiing to keep our immune systems in tip-top condition. while the immune system does a rather good job at protecting us, there are many things we can do to help keep such a complex system working effectively.

A Healthy Lifestyle:
Every part of the body, including the immune syetem, functions better with a healthy lifestyle. Maitaining a healthy weight, eating plenty of fruit and vegetables, having a regular exercise routine, moderating your alcohol intake, and getting enough sleep can all contribute to a healthy body.

A Healthy Gut:
70-80% of the body's immune cells are found in the gut, so maintaining a healthy digestive system can directly affect how the immune system responds to threats. Probiotics can help to restore healthy levels of gut bacteria. Probiotics can be found in fermented foods such as kimchi,sauerraut or cultured yoghurt, but are also available as a supplement. Prebiotics and dietary fibre are equally as important to maintain a suitable environment for the healthy bacteria.

Stress:
Short term or mild stress is something we all go through, but ongoing or chronic stress can play havoc with your body and weaken the immune system. Stress can affect the digestive system and heart health, both of which contribute to healthy immune system.

Herbs:
Herbs that are known to support the function of the immune system include echinacea, elderberry, astragalus, garlic. These can be taken in capsules or tablet form,tincture form,tisane (tea or infusion) form or by adding them to your diet.
Terms like immune-boosting can be confusing. We don't want an immune system to be functioning above or below the capacity it's supposed to be.
We want it to be functioning effectively and to not be hampered by things like a lack of sleep, junk food, smoking, obesity, alcohol and drug use, which can all have a negative impact on the immune system and its ability to protect us.
